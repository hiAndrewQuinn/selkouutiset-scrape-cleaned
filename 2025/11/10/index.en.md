## Healthcare

First, the topic is healthcare.

Finnish healthcare needs to be completely changed. This is what University of Helsinki professor **Kristiina Patja** says.

Kristiina Patja says the healthcare system is inefficient and unfair. Employees can access treatment quickly through occupational health. At the same time, sick elderly people sometimes have to wait a long time for treatment.

According to the expert, Finnish healthcare needs to be completely changed.

Professor Kristiina Patja says that public healthcare and occupational health together cost Finland too much money.

## Pekka Halonen

Next, culture.

Paintings by Finnish painter **Pekka Halonen** are on display in Paris.

Halonen's paintings are in the Petit Palais museum.

Pekka Halonen was born in 1865. He painted a lot of nature and life in the countryside. Many of Halonen's paintings feature snow.

Pekka Halonen studied in Paris when he was young.

Paintings by painter Pekka Halonen are on display in Paris. This is the first time that many of Halonen's paintings have been displayed in a Parisian museum. The exhibition is open until February.

## Ringette

Now sports.

Finland has won the world championship in ringette.

Finland defeated Canada 7–3 in the second final. On Saturday, Finland won the first final against Canada 4–2. The games were played in Lahti.

This is the 9th time in a row that Finland has won the World Ringette gold medal.

Finland has won the world championship in ringette.

Ringette is a racquet game played on ice. Ringette was developed in Canada for women and girls, but today men also play ringette.
