## Masking at protests

First, the news about disguise.

A discussion has begun in Finland about wearing masks at protests.

The reason is the far-right May Day march in Tampere. Masked protesters assaulted a counter-protester.

The attackers cannot be identified because of the masks.

There is currently a debate in Finland about whether masking should be banned.

Prime Minister **Petteri Orpo** says he supports a ban on masks at protests.

Minister of the Interior **Mari Rantanen** says that covering the face should always be banned in public places.

Police Inspector **Konsta Arvelin** says that there is no need to ban masked people completely. It is enough to ease the police's right to order masks to be removed.

## Blueberry plant disease

Finally, news about plant disease.

A new plant disease has spread to blueberries in Finland. The disease is called *blueberry spot disease*.

Biologist **Jouni Issakainen** is studying blueberry shoots. He collects diseased leaves in a research box.

Last summer, 400 sightings of the blue-spotted goby were made from different parts of Finland.

The disease destroys the tops of the blueberry and reduces the blueberry harvest.

The disease is not dangerous to humans.

A new plant disease, blueberry spot blotch, has spread to blueberries in Finland.

Researchers are asking for observations about the disease. These can be reported online to the laji.fi service.
