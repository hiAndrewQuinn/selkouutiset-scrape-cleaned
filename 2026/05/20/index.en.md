## Petty theft can land you in court

Then the news about pickpocketing, or petty theft.

The Helsinki District Court is now handling a lot of petty theft cases.

There is a backlog in the courts because the Helsinki police started investigating petty thefts. Previously, the police did not investigate thefts that were really small.

Now you can be prosecuted if you steal, for example, a chocolate bar from a store.

The Helsinki District Court is overloaded with petty theft cases, which take up a lot of the judges' time.

The head of the Helsinki District Court says the situation is frustrating.

For example, the theft of a water bottle has been dealt with in court. The bottle cost 50 cents.

## Young people with a foreign background often experience discrimination

Finally, news about discrimination against young people.

Young people with a foreign background say in the survey that they are discriminated against more than before.

Upper secondary school students responded to a school health survey conducted by the Finnish Institute for Health and Welfare (THL).

In the survey, half of young people with a foreign background said that they had experienced discrimination at school and during their free time.
