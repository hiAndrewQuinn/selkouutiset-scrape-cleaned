## Finland changes nuclear weapons law

First, news about nuclear weapons in Finland.

Finland is changing the law that restricts nuclear weapons.

The law now prohibits all nuclear weapons in Finland.

The government wants to change the law. The government wants to allow nuclear weapons to be brought into Finland if they are used to defend Finland.

Many parties in Parliament are criticizing the government's plan. The parties are demanding that decisions on nuclear weapons be made together.

Finland is changing the law that restricts nuclear weapons.

## Finland evacuates from the Middle East

Finns are being brought home from the war zone in the Middle East.

The Finnish government is organizing a flight that will depart from Oman on Sunday. The ticket costs approximately 1,200 euros.

600 Finns wanted to board the flight. Only about 160 can fit on the flight.

As many as 3,000 Finns were in the Middle East when the war in Iran began last weekend. They were stranded in the region as flights were canceled and airports were closed due to airstrikes.

Finns will be brought home from the Middle East war zone on Sunday.

## EU monitors poverty in Finland

Then the news about poverty.

More and more young adults are at risk of poverty and exclusion. More than one in three young adults is at risk.

Poverty is particularly threatening for those under 30 who live alone.

Finland has come under EU scrutiny due to poverty. There is too much unemployment in Finland, and Finns have too little money to spend compared to other EU countries. Health services can also be difficult to access in Finland.

More and more young adults are at risk of falling into poverty and marginalization.

## The suspect in the Vantaa fire has been arrested

Finally, some news about the Vantaa fire.

Police have arrested a 71-year-old man in connection with a fire in Pähkinärinte, Vantaa.

Police suspect the man of 5 murders. Police also suspect that the man intentionally started the fire.

2 adults and 3 children died in the fire.
