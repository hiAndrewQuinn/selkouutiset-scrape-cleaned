## Military drones

First, news about drones.

There is a discussion in Finland about Ukrainian military aircraft, or drones.

Two Ukrainian military drones fell in Finland on Sunday.

The fall sites were isolated.

The military blew up another drone because it might have contained explosives.

The second drone broke apart when it fell.

There is a discussion in Finland about Ukrainian military drones.

Foreign Minister **Elina Valtonen** says that the reason for the drones is Russia, because it has attacked Ukraine.

Finland does not prohibit Ukraine from sending drones towards Russia.

Ukraine has apologized for its drones falling in Finland.

## Unemployment among young women

Now the news about unemployment among young women.

Unemployment among young women is increasing.

It grew more than unemployment in other age groups last year.

**Taru Kyllönen** from Vantaa has been looking for work since December.

Unemployment among young women is now higher than unemployment among young men.

For example, the social and health sectors employ many women, but many jobs have been cut in the sector.

When the employment situation is poor, young people are the first to suffer.

Taru Kyllönen says that finding a job seems difficult.

"I'm slowly starting to feel like I'm going to have to apply for those jobs when everyone just says, 'We didn't choose you.'"

However, Kyllönen continues to look for work.

## Communion breads or öyläts

Finally, a word about communion bread.

This is the bakery of the Deaconess Institute in Kallio, Helsinki.

Communion breads, or öylätti, have been made here for 100 years.

The bakery produces approximately 800,000 pastries every year.

The breads are handmade from wheat flour and water.

The most important thing is the appearance and the beautiful crucifix pattern on the bread.

Baker **Pia Greus** says that the pastries don't taste good at all.

"They don't taste like much, but that's not the most important thing for us."

The Deaconess Institute's bakery has been making communion bread for 100 years.
